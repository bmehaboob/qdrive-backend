How to Use QDrive

A clean, simple workflow directly inside QGIS

1

Install & Authenticate

Install the QDrive plugin from the QGIS repository. Open the QDrive panel (via the Web menu or toolbar) and click Login. A browser window will open asking you to authenticate with your Google account. Once granted, return to QGIS.

2

Browse Your Drive

Your Google Drive files will load in the panel. Double-click folders to navigate. Use the Search bar to find specific files globally, or click the ⭐ Starred button to filter your favorite datasets.

3

Download & Open Layers

Select one or more GIS files (e.g., .shp, .gpkg, .geojson, .tif) and click ⬇ Download. The files are securely cached on your computer and automatically added to your QGIS map canvas as layers.

4

Upload & Organize

To save local data to Drive, click ⬆ Upload to select files, or simply Drag & Drop them from Windows Explorer directly into the QDrive panel. Companion files (like .shx, .dbf for shapefiles) are detected and uploaded automatically.

5

Sync & Autosave (Pro feature)

Right-click a folder to Sync it locally. If you are editing a Drive-hosted vector layer in QGIS, turn on Autosave. Every time you save your edits in QGIS, the changes are silently pushed back to Google Drive in the background.

What QDrive accesses

QDrive requests only the minimum Google permissions it needs to function:

  • Google Drive — read & write: Browse your files, download GIS layers, upload files to Drive
  • Email address — read only: Display your logged-in account identity in the plugin
